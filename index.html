Self Driving Cars

Our Point of View

We should not have self-driving cars, not only are they dangerous; they are at risk of cyber-attacks, destroying our jobs and harming the general population. We cannot allow they unsightly devices on our roads so that we protect our family’s and loved one. This website will list our key concerns about this issue.

Can cars be Hacked?

Did you know that self-driving cars can hacked? 2015, two researchers from group IOActive discovered a way to hack into a jeep through the internet-connected entertainment system of the car into the low-level system which controls the vehicle. It allowed them to send commands to brake or steer the car wirelessly, this prompted a mass recall of all the affected models.

To further showcase these shocking revelations the pair presented an updated version at Defcon, a cyber-security conference, in 2016 that illustrated just how much more they could have done after slowly reverse engineering the Jeep’s low-level system. If this wasn’t enough, researchers at the University of South Carolina in the US, China’s Zhejiang University, and the Chinese security firm Qihoo 360 demonstrated that they could jam various sensors in Tesla model S’, making objects invisible to its navigation system which could result in a deadly collision.

The method that these people are using is connecting a device to a car using the can bus standard, after being widely embarrassed by their failure to consider security at all the CAN bus, which was designed in the 1980s, has no concept of authentication and can easily be exploited.

In the expert opinions of professional security researchers from the point of view of a hacker having just one sensor makes it much easier to fake a signal or event to fool the car into doing something such as crashing.

Why would anyone want to hack a self-driving car, knowing that it could result in a death? One reason is that widespread deployment of self-driving cars could result in a lot of unemployed people. These people are not going to be very happy or anarchist who just like seeing the world burn.

Job Losses

Over 3.8 million people drive trucks, delivery vans and an estimated 1 million people drive part time for Uber, there are also 180,000 taxi drivers, 500,000 school bus drivers, and 160,000 transit bus drivers. This includes truck driving, which is most common profession in 29 states in the us, this employs about 1.7 million people. How would you like it if you lose your job to autonomous machines? .

Trucks are likely to be the first type of self-driving vehicles to hit the roads, which would put all of these workers in a tough spot. Job losses at a rate of 25,000 a month, or 300,000 a year, according to a report from Goldman Sachs Economics Research.

Marc Andreessen, the co-founder of venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz and early web pioneer, said “It’s a recurring panic. This happens every 25 or 50 years, people get all amped up about ‘machines are going to take all the jobs’ and it never happens”. What he never considered is that they are already trialing this technology.

TROLLEY PROBLEM

There is a trolley running down a track. There are five people tied to the track and are unable to escape. The trolley will ultimately kill the five people. However, there is a lever that will change the trolleys path but on this new path has one person strapped to the track.

What would you choose?

If you pull this lever the trolley will avoid killing the five people, yet you will be killing one person on the other track. What would you choose? Kill one person to save five or let nature take its course?
